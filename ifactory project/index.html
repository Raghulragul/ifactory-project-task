SCROLLING IN WEB DESIGGN

THE LONG & THE SHORT OF IT

LONG SCROLL: THE GOOD STUFF

VISUAL CREATIVITY

There's more visual freedom in parallax website design, Rather than
focusing on block style information architecture,you can create
refreshing and engaging visuals such as animation or beautifual
photography, that act as a continum,ultimately inspiring users to
scroll further down the page.

S TORY-TELLING

OPPORTUNITIES

Do you want to tell a story with your product or service?
Long-scrolling lends itself well to a narrative and
encourages meaningful interaction from users

SIMPLE INTERFACE FOR

SMART DEVICE USERS

Mobile and tablets have evolved the way we engage in website. it's
now less about clicking and more about scrolling ,swiping and tapping,
long-scrolling websites take away the barrier of complex navigation,
providing a responsive, user-friendly experience for mobile users.

PUNCHY, STRAIGHT TO

THE POINT MESSAGING

Parallex website design is a grate solution for us time-poor
website browsers,as it forces business to convey their
message and vision into one dynamic web design page.

TASIER FOR USERS TO

REALISE THEIR GOAL

There days,scrolling is so simple that it's become an
instinctive action, whereas clicking through to another page
requiers thought and motivation. A single page website
allows user to identify their purpose on the website quicker,
than needing to site through multiple pages.

LONG SCROOL: THE NOT SO GOOD STUFF

YOU NEED TO BE SEARCH ENGINE SAVVY


One of the biggest problems that parallex websites face is that
they are not SEO friendly.why?when you limit the number of
pages in your website, Google has fewer pages to index and
negatively impacts your standing on the search engine results
page(SERP).Luckily there are ways around this.

SLOWER LOAD TIMES

50% of website users bounce off the page if it hasn't loaded in 3-5 seconds. So the
more high resolution imagery and animations you include, yhe longer it takes to
load the pages. when writing this article,we tested some popular parallax
websites, and found that the ones with complex visuals took up to
30 seconds to load on a mobile device, whereas simple and minimalist long-scroll websites
loaded within a few seconds.

AWKWARD NAVIGATION

Instead of hitting the "back" button, users must scroll back
up the page which can be frustrating for users who want to
refer back to a certain element. An easy way around this is
to include top navigation and have each element anchored
to a category or section
